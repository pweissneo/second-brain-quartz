# Frontier Exploration - AI-Generated Content Verification

## The Gap

The Seed covers:
- Source citation requirements
- Confidence markers
- Procedural knowledge verification
- AI-optimized knowledge structures

But there's no explicit guidance on: **How to verify that content generated by AI assistants is accurate before adding it to the vault.**

## Why This Matters

As AI assistants become partners in knowledge base building:
1. AI can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect information (hallucinations)
2. AI may cite sources that don't exist or misquote real sources
3. AI-generated content needs the same verification as human-generated content
4. Without verification rules, the vault could accumulate inaccurate knowledge

## The Problem for AI Agents

When an AI generates a note:
1. AI writes: "Vitamin D helps with depression" with a citation
2. How does the human verify this is accurate?
3. The Seed says "cite sources" but doesn't say "verify AI-cited sources"
4. The AI might hallucinate the citation entirely

## Current Guidance Gaps

### Gap 1: No Verification Workflow
The Seed has `verification-status: unverified | tested | community-validated` but:
- Who verifies AI-generated content?
- What's the minimum verification required?
- Can AI self-verify, or does it need human oversight?

### Gap 2: Source Hallucination Detection
AI might produce citations like:
- "Study published in Journal of X (2023)" that doesn't exist
- Misquoted findings
- Real studies with incorrect conclusions attributed

### Gap 3: Confidence Appropriate for AI Generation
If AI generates content with 80% confidence, what should the note's confidence marker be?

## Proposed Rules

### Rule 1: AI-Generated Content Tagging

**Rule:** Notes partially or fully generated by AI must include `author-type: AI-assisted | AI-first-pass | human-only` in frontmatter.
**Why:** Readers (and other AIs) need to know the provenance of the content to assess reliability.
**Test:** Can you identify which notes were AI-generated? Is each marked appropriately?

### Rule 2: Minimum Verification for AI Content

**Rule:** AI-generated notes require at minimum ONE form of verification before marking as verified:
- Cross-reference with trusted source
- Human review acknowledgment
- Comparison with established databases

**Why:** AI hallucinations can appear confident but be completely wrong. Verification is non-optional.
**Test:** For AI-generated notes, is there verification documentation? Can you trace how accuracy was confirmed?

### Rule 3: Source Verification Requirement

**Rule:** For AI-generated notes, at least one cited source must be spot-checked (open the URL, verify the claim exists).
**Why:** AI can hallucinate citations entirely. Spot-checking prevents false attribution.
**Test:** Can you verify at least one source in AI-generated notes is real and accurately cited?

### Rule 4: Confidence Adjustment

**Rule:** AI-generated content should start at `confidence: emerging` unless verified by a human, regardless of how the AI rates its own confidence.
**Why:** AI confidence is not calibrated to human reliability. A confident AI hallucination is more dangerous than an AI admitting uncertainty.
**Test:** Do AI-generated notes show appropriate confidence (not "high" without human verification)?

## Verification Workflow

### For AI-Assisted Notes (Human writes, AI helps):
1. Human creates draft with AI assistance
2. Mark as `author-type: AI-assisted`
3. Human verifies key claims
4. Set confidence based on verification level

### For AI-First-Pass Notes (AI writes, human reviews):
1. AI generates initial note
2. Mark as `author-type: AI-first-pass`
3. Human performs spot-check verification
4. Update to `author-type: human-verified` after verification
5. Set confidence appropriately

### For Critical Domain Content:
- Medical, legal, financial AI-generated content requires expert review
- Mark with `review-required: expert` for domain-critical content

## Edge Cases

### Edge Case 1: AI Improving Existing Notes
An AI suggests edits to a human-written note. Is this "AI-generated"?
- Solution: Track diffs, verify significant changes, maintain human as primary author

### Edge Case 2: AI Generating Based on Verified Sources
AI synthesizes from verified sources - is this still "AI-generated"?
- Solution: Mark as AI-synthesized but note source verification status

### Edge Case 3: Multiple AI Iterations
Note goes through AI → human → AI → human. What's the author type?
- Solution: Use `author-type: collaborative` with iteration history in notes

### Edge Case 4: AI Confidence Calibration
AI says "99% confident" - should confidence reflect this?
- Solution: No. AI confidence != reliable confidence. Default to emerging unless verified.

## Interaction with Other Rules

- **Verification status**: AI-generated notes should show verification process
- **Confidence markers**: AI confidence should not override appropriate skepticism
- **Source validation**: AI sources need extra verification, not less
- **Procedural content**: AI-generated procedures need testing before "tested" status

## Test for AI Agents

Given a note that may be AI-generated:
1. Is there `author-type` frontmatter?
2. Is there verification documentation?
3. Can you spot-check at least one source?
4. Is confidence appropriate for verification level?
5. For critical domains, is expert review documented?
