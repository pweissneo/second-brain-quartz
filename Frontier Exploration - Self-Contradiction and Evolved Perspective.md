# Frontier Exploration: Self-Contradiction and Evolved Perspective

## The Problem

Knowledge bases typically handle external contradictions (Source A says X, Source B says Y) and superseded knowledge (Old understanding was X, new understanding is Y). But there's a gap: **when you contradict yourself**.

Examples:
- "I used to recommend approach X but now recommend approach Y"
- "My view on topic Z changed after experiencing failure"
- "What I thought was true in 2024 proved incorrect in 2025"

This is different from:
- External contradiction (two sources disagree)
- Superseded knowledge (field evolved, not just you)
- Deprecated notes (replaced by better version)

## Why It Matters

1. **Personal knowledge compounds** - If you don't track your own evolution, you lose the learning
2. **Context preservation** - Knowing *why* you changed helps others understand
3. **Prevents repeated mistakes** - Documenting what you used to believe helps avoid backsliding
