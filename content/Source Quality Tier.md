# Source Quality Tier

> In domains with established evidence hierarchies, capture source quality alongside confidence levels.

## The Problem

The Seed's confidence markers (high/emerging/disputed/obsolete) indicate how certain we are about knowledge — but they don't indicate the *quality of the source* where the knowledge came from. A claim from a systematic review is fundamentally different from anecdotal opinion, even if both have "high" confidence.

## Evidence Tiers

### Medical/Scientific Domains
| Tier | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Systematic Review | Synthesizes multiple RCTs | Cochrane reviews |
| RCT | Randomized controlled trial | Clinical trials |
| Observational Study | Cohort/case-control studies | Epidemiological data |
| Case Report | Single patient observations | Clinical anecdotes |
| Expert Opinion | Specialist judgment | Editorials, reviews |
| Anecdote | Personal experience | Case studies, stories |

### Legal Domains
| Tier | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Case Law | Court decisions |
| Statutory Law | Legislation |
| Regulatory Guidance | Agency rules |
| Legal Commentary | Expert analysis |

### General Knowledge
| Tier | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Primary Source | Direct evidence, original publication |
| Secondary Source | Analysis, synthesis |
| Tertiary Source | Summaries, encyclopedias |

## When to Use Source Tier

Source tier matters most when:
- Knowledge will inform decisions with significant consequences
- Multiple sources of varying quality exist
- Readers need to assess reliability themselves

## Seed Reference

The Seed includes this guidance:

> **Rule:** For knowledge domains with established evidence hierarchies (medicine, law, science), capture source quality tier in addition to confidence levels.
> **Why:** Source quality (systematic reviews > RCTs > case reports) is distinct from confidence (how certain we are about the knowledge). Treating all sources equally loses critical reliability information in high-stakes domains.
