# Knowledge Retention

Knowledge retention is the ability to maintain and recall information over time. In a second brain system, retention happens through two mechanisms:

## Passive Retention
- Well-linked notes are easier to find later
- Graph structure surfaces related concepts
- Search and backlinks enable rediscovery

## Active Retention
- [[The Forgetting Curve]] for memorization
- Writing notes in your own words (encoding)
- Teaching concepts to others (elaboration)
- Cross-domain connections (interleaving)

## The forgetting Curve

Ebbinghaus discovered that without reinforcement, ~75% of new information is forgotten within 24 hours. A well-maintained knowledge graph counters this by:

1. **Redundancy** - Multiple paths to same information
2. **Context** - Links provide retrieval cues
3. **Active Use** - Writing, connecting, reviewing

## Best Practices

- Review notes before they become stale
- Update outdated information promptly
- Create [[Note Lifecycle Management]] that evolve over time
