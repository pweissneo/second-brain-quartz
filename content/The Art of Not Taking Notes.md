# The Art of Not Taking Notes

Not every piece of information deserves a note. Knowing what to skip is as important as knowing what to capture.

## When NOT to Take Notes

### 1. Easily Retrievable Information
- Phone numbers, addresses, quick facts
- Anything a web search can find in 5 seconds
- Documentation that won't disappear

### 2. Ephemeral Conversations
- Meeting notes that won't be referenced again
- Quick status updates
- One-off questions answered once

### 3. Other People's Private Info
- Don't become a data hoarder's assistant
- Information not actionable for you

### 4. Duplicate Knowledge
- If you already have a note covering this, update it instead
- Check with `graph_search` before creating

### 5. Momentum Capture
- Don't pause a deep conversation to take notes
- Capture the essence later, stay in the flow

## The Reverse Test

Before creating a note, ask:
- Will I need this in 6 months?
- Does this connect to something I already know?
- Is this my insight or someone else's?

If no → skip. Your second brain will thank you.
