# Frontier Exploration - Collaborative Knowledge Creation

> How to capture and structure knowledge that is built jointly by multiple contributors

## The Problem

Traditional knowledge management assumes a single author or at least a coherent editorial voice. But modern knowledge work increasingly involves:
- Team research projects with multiple experts
- AI-human co-creation where different contributions blend
- Community wikis with multiple editors
- Collaborative note-taking in meetings

The Seed has no rules for handling knowledge where:
- Multiple contributors have different expertise levels
- Contributors may disagree on the "right" way to capture something
- Attribution matters for credibility or accountability
- Contributors join and leave over time

## Key Questions

### 1. Attribution

**When does it matter?**
- Academic research (credit for ideas)
- Professional contexts (accountability for advice)
- Open source style projects (who wrote what)

**When it doesn't matter:**
- Purely personal knowledge consolidation
- Synthesized knowledge that transcends sources

**Test:** Can you articulate why attribution matters for this note?

### 2. Handling Disagreement

Different scenarios:

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Factual disagreement | Present both views with evidence, mark confidence |
| Methodological disagreement | Present alternatives with applicability context |
| Style/preference disagreement | No need to resolve - document both |

**Test:** For notes where contributors disagreed, is the disagreement documented?

### 3. Consensus vs Plurality

Not all knowledge needs consensus. Distinguish:
- **Consensus required**: Safety-critical information, core principles
- **Majority OK**: Best practices where multiple approaches work
- **Plurality OK**: Opinions, preferences, style choices

**Test:** Can you categorize the note's knowledge by which threshold applies?

### 4. Contribution Tracking

Options:
- **Full attribution**: Every claim tagged with contributor
- **Summary attribution**: Note-level byline with major contributors
- **Implicit**: No explicit tracking, assume single voice

**Test:** For collaborative notes, can you identify who contributed what?

### 5. Lifecycle in Collaborative Contexts

Collaborative notes have unique challenges:
- Contributors may leave (knowledge orphaned)
- New contributors may change direction
- Historical contributions may become controversial

**Test:** For notes with departed contributors: Is the knowledge still maintainable?

## Seed Rule Proposal

**Rule:** For knowledge with multiple contributors, document contribution model and handle disagreement explicitly.
**Why:** Unstructured collaboration leads to incoherent notes that mix incompatible viewpoints without signaling the mix.
**Test:** (1) Can you identify all contributors? (2) For any claim, can you trace it to a source? (3) If contributors disagreed, is the disagreement visible?

## Examples

### Academic Research Collaboration
Multiple researchers with different expertise contribute to a shared understanding. Attribution at section level, consensus on core findings, plurality on interpretations.

### AI-Human Co-Creation
Human provides domain expertise, AI structures and synthesizes. Attribution becomes complex - the AI's contributions are derivative but the synthesis is new. Treat as "synthesized" with human as primary source.

### Team Meeting Notes
Multiple attendees contribute perspectives. Note-level attribution (who was present), synthesize into coherent narrative, flag areas where perspectives differed.
