# Retrieval Practice

The most effective learning technique: forcing yourself to recall information.

## Why Retrieval Works

Retrieval strengthens memory pathways:
- **Passive re-reading** = weak encoding
- **Active recall** = strong memory

> "The effort of retrieving learning is where the learning happens."

## How to Practice Retrieval

### 1. Flashcards
- Convert atomic notes to flashcards
- Use spaced repetition (see [[The Forgetting Curve]])
- Focus on connections, not just facts

### 2. Closed-Book Notes
- Try to write from memory
- Check against your notes
- Fill gaps

### 3. Teaching
- Explain a concept without looking
- The [[Writing as Thinking|Feynman Technique]]
- Identify gaps in understanding

### 4. Practice Problems
- Apply concepts to new scenarios
- Test understanding, not just recall

## Retrieval in Your Knowledge Base

1. **Review sessions** - Close notes, try to reconstruct
2. **Flashcard creation** - From atomic notes
3. **Quiz yourself** - Use Dataview to generate questions
4. **Teach others** - Share [[Knowledge Sharing|knowledge]] to verify

## Common Mistakes

- **Passive reading** - Just highlighting = no learning
- **Too soon** - Retrieval after 5 minutes < retrieval after 1 day
- **Too easy** - Don't use multiple choice; use free recall
