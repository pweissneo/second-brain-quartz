# The Knowledge Portfolio

Diversify your knowledge like financial investments.

## Financial Portfolio

Different assets:
- Stocks (high risk/reward)
- Bonds (stable)
- Real estate (tangible)
- Cash (liquid)

Diversification reduces risk.

## Knowledge Portfolio

Different knowledge types:
- **Core** - Foundations (30%)
  - Foundational principles
  - Key frameworks
  
- **Domain** - Your field (40%)
  - Specialization
  - Expertise
  
- **Adjacent** - Connected fields (20%)
  - Cross-pollination
  - Analogies
  
- **Exploratory** - New frontiers (10%)
  - Emerging trends
  - Experiments

## Why Diversify

1. **Resilience** - One field down = others still valuable
2. **Innovation** - Adjacent fields create insights
3. **Opportunity** - Exploratory leads to new areas

## The Knowledge Base Advantage

Unlike financial portfolios:
- No capital required
- Knowledge is non-rivalrous
- Dividends compound forever

## Rebalancing

Periodically adjust:
- Too much passive consumption → Process more
- Too narrow → Explore adjacent
- Too scattered → Deepen core
