# The Forgetting Curve and Its Implications

Understanding memory decay to optimize your second brain.

## The Science (Ebbinghaus, 1885)

Hermann Ebbinghaus discovered that information decays rapidly:
- 25% remembered after 1 day
- 33% remembered after 2 days
- 28% remembered after 6 days
- 25% remembered after 31 days

This is the **forgetting curve**.

## The Implications for Knowledge Management

### 1. Review Timing
Review notes BEFORE forgetting:
- First review: 1 day after creating
- Second review: 3 days later
- Third review: 7 days later
- Fourth review: 14 days later
- Fifth review: 30 days later

This is the basis of spaced repetition (see above).

### 2. Active Engagement
Passive re-reading does little. Active retrieval is key:
- Close your notes and try to recall
- Test yourself with flashcards
- Apply concepts to new situations

### How to Practice Retrieval

The most effective learning technique is forcing yourself to recall information.

#### 1. Flashcards
- Convert atomic notes to flashcards
- Use spaced repetition (see above)
- Focus on connections, not just facts

#### 2. Closed-Book Notes
- Try to write from memory
- Check against your notes
- Fill gaps

#### 3. Teaching
- Explain a concept without looking
- The Feynman Technique
- Identify gaps in understanding

#### 4. Practice Problems
- Apply concepts to new scenarios
- Test understanding, not just recall

### Common Retrieval Mistakes
- **Passive reading** — Just highlighting = no learning
- **Too soon** — Retrieval after 5 minutes < retrieval after 1 day
- **Too easy** — Don't use multiple choice; use free recall

### 3. Meaningful Connections
Links in your graph provide retrieval cues:
- When you see a related note, it triggers recall
- More connections = more retrieval paths
- This is why [[Linking Principle]] matters

### 4. Regular Maintenance
Per [[Self-Improvement Cycle]] and [[Graph Maintenance]]:
- Review notes before they go stale
- Update outdated information
- Strengthen weak connections

## Counteracting the Curve

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Meaningful encoding | Slower decay |
| Spaced review | Extends retention |
| Active retrieval | Strengthens memory |
| Multiple contexts | Richer encoding |

### Active vs Passive Retention

In a second brain system, retention happens through two mechanisms:

**Passive Retention:**
- Well-linked notes are easier to find later
- Graph structure surfaces related concepts
- Search and backlinks enable rediscovery

**Active Retention:**
- Spaced repetition review
- Writing notes in your own words (encoding)
- Teaching concepts to others (elaboration)
- Cross-domain connections (interleaving)

### Knowledge Base Application

1. **Create notes** with strong connections (more retrieval cues)
2. **Review systematically** using spaced repetition
3. **Update regularly** to reinforce memory
4. **Link extensively** to create retrieval network

### Retrieval in Your Knowledge Base

1. **Review sessions** - Close notes, try to reconstruct
2. **Flashcard creation** - From atomic notes
3. **Quiz yourself** - Use Dataview to generate questions
4. **Teach others** - Share knowledge to verify
